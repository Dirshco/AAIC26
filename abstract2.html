Background

Behavioral and psychological symptoms of dementia (BPSD) are prevalent in long-term and post-acute care (PALTC) settings and may intensify during environmental disruptions or emergencies.

The CMS Emergency Preparedness Rule emphasizes staff training, communication strategies, and resident-centered responses — yet frontline caregivers often lack rapid-access tools that translate these principles into actionable, nonpharmacologic guidance.

Clinical Problem

Staff lack real-time de-escalation tools during emergencies
BPSD triggers amplify during environmental disruptions

Methods

We developed Crisis Card AI, a web-based prototype providing person-centered, nonpharmacologic responses to BPSD — aligned with CMS Emergency Preparedness and PALTC care principles.

Device-Agnostic: No installation; works on any browser
60+ Scenarios: Curated rules-based BPSD scenarios
Constrained LLM: Fixed schema · Grade 4 reading level
Zero PHI: No data collection or transmission

Ethics / Disclosure

Non-human subjects research
IRB review not required; no PHI collected or analyzed
Tested by two geriatricians

Conclusions

This prototype demonstrates that CMS nonpharmacologic dementia care guidance can be delivered in real time at the point of care.
A constrained prompt produces consistent, simplified crisis response outputs suitable for emergency disruption scenarios.
The prototyp have safety constraints and aligned with PALTC nonpharmacologic care principles.

Future Directions

Formal usability testing with frontline PALTC caregivers and interdisciplinary teams to evaluate real-world acceptability and workflow integration
Longitudinal evaluation of psychotropic medication reliance as a system-level outcome measure following Crisis Card AI adoption
